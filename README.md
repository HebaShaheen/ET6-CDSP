## Project Overview
Over the past several years, online learning has become one of the most significant transformations in global education. From the sudden shift during the COVID-19 pandemic to the continued use of digital platforms today, students across all educational levels schools and universities have had to adapt quickly to new learning environments. This transition created opportunities for flexible, accessible education, but it also exposed major challenges in engagement, academic performance, and digital readiness.

## [M2: Data Collection](https://github.com/HebaShaheen/ET6-CDSP/tree/main/1_datasets)
I used three publicly available datasets collected from Kaggle, all based on student responses related to online learning. The datasets cover different aspects of students‚Äô online education experiences, including academic performance, adaptability to online learning, and time utilization during the COVID-19 period.

The datasets provides information on student learning environments, technology access, engagement, and academic performance before and during online learning. The Students Adaptability Level in Online Education dataset focuses on students‚Äô adaptability and examines the role of infrastructure, devices, and financial conditions. The COVID-19 and its Impact on Students dataset captures students‚Äô daily habits and perceived productivity during online learning.

Together, these datasets offer a comprehensive view of students‚Äô online learning experiences across different contexts and time periods.

## [M3: Data Preparation](https://github.com/HebaShaheen/ET6-CDSP/tree/main/2_data_preparation)

## [M4: Data Exploration](https://github.com/HebaShaheen/ET6-CDSP/tree/main/3_data_exploration)

I explored three cleaned datasets related to online education, adaptability, and time utilization. Building on the prepared data from the data preparation stage, I used descriptive statistics and visualizations to understand distributions, patterns, and relationships within each dataset.

## [M5: Data Analysis](https://github.com/HebaShaheen/ET6-CDSP/tree/main/4_data_analysis)

I built it on the exploratory data to examine patterns related to student engagement, adaptability, satisfaction, and performance across three datasets. I analyzed each dataset separately, using descriptive statistics, visualizations, and predictive modeling to understand key trends and relationships in online learning experiences.

Overall, this analysis showed that engagement, access to resources, and learning conditions are more influential in shaping online learning outcomes than demographic characteristics alone.
